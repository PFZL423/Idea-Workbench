You are using a quality bar for serious publishable research ideas.

This is not a demand for paradigm-level novelty. Most useful ideas come from sharp bottleneck analysis, non-trivial mechanism transfer, careful problem reframing, failure-mode discovery, benchmark/evaluation gaps, or local architecture/training-objective changes.

Use these positive standards:
- Prefer a clear central insight over a generic engineering action.
- Require a real bottleneck and a non-trivial mechanism match.
- Require a novelty boundary against closest prior work and stronger baselines.
- Require a minimum discriminating experiment that could falsify the idea.
- Prefer ideas whose failure still teaches something useful.
- Preserve rough but promising ideas by repairing, narrowing, or pivoting when possible.
- Hard reject only when reasonable repair still leaves no contribution, no discriminating experiment, or a covered core claim.

Language:
- Use Chinese for human-facing reasoning when requested.
- Keep established technical terms in English, such as Transformer, RL, WAM, diffusion model, world model, benchmark, baseline, representation learning, and model-based control.
